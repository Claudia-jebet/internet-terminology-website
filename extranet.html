Extranet

Extended Private Network — Controlled external access

What is an Extranet?

An Extranet is an extension of an organisation's Intranet that allows controlled, limited access to selected external parties such as suppliers, business partners, or clients. It is more open than an Intranet but more restricted than the public Internet.

Example: A company allowing its suppliers to log in and check inventory levels, or a university giving alumni access to certain resources.

Extranet = Intranet + controlled external access. It sits between the private Intranet and the public Internet.
